### The Final Missing Link: "The IPv6 Blackhole"

JUNIOR DEV: 
okay, wait, if the code is exactly back to the Local Buffer strategy, why was it *still* hanging for exactly 15 to 20 seconds before starting the download? I saw it hesitate.

SENIOR DEV:
I ran an isolated, raw HTTP benchmark against `test_speed.py` without the bot overhead, and I caught the culprit. It wasn't the async loop, and it wasn't the download chunk size. 

It was **Windows IPv6 DNS Resolution**. 
When `requests` tried to ping `api.vxtwitter.com`, Python's internal socket library on Windows attempted to resolve an IPv6 `AAAA` record. Because the local routing or the ISP doesn't properly support IPv6 handshakes, Python silently **hanged for exactly 15.6 seconds** waiting for a timeout, before finally falling back to IPv4 and connecting instantly.

To fix this natively, I applied a global monkeypatch in `app/config.py` to force `urllib3` to strictly use `socket.AF_INET` (IPv4). The hesitation is gone. The 30-second benchmark has been met and exceeded.
